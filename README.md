# 🧠 The Second Brain Starter Kit

The adaptable skeleton for an AI second brain — the thing that stops your AI forgetting you every time you close the tab. Free. Take it, flex it to *your* why, make it yours.

> **Read this first:** don't copy a second brain because you saw one online. Build it because you know what it's *for*. This repo is the **universal layer** — the structure and habits that work for anyone. What you build *on top* (your tools, your agents, your automations) is yours, and it should answer *your* why, not mine.
> *(The thinking behind this is in my post: "Most people build an AI second brain wrong — here's what actually works.")*

## The four rooms
Everything your AI needs answers one of four questions. So your vault has four folders:

| Room | Folder | It answers |
|------|--------|-----------|
| 🧍 WHO | `Context/` | Who am I? (you, your work, your goals, how you like the AI to work) |
| 🛠️ WORK | `Projects/` | What am I building? (one per active project) |
| 🧠 KNOW | `Intelligence/` | What have I learned? (decisions + why, people, meetings) |
| 📓 LOG | `Daily/` | What just happened? (one dated note per working day) |

**Who you are · what you're working on · what you know · what happened.** That's the whole system.

## Get started — how to actually fill it in

**1. Get the kit.** Clone this repo, or hit the green **`Code`** button above → **Download ZIP**, and unzip it.

**2. Fill it in — two ways:**

🤖 **Let AI do it (fastest — and the best demo of the whole idea).** Open this folder in [Claude Code](https://claude.com/claude-code) (or any AI that can read the files) and paste this:

> *Read every template in this folder. Then interview me one question at a time to fill in `Context/about-me.md` — ask about who I am, my work, what I'm focused on right now, who I serve, and how I like you to work with me. When we're done, save the completed file.*

It'll ask you a handful of questions and write the file for you. (Using AI to set up your AI's memory — that's the whole point.)

✍️ **Or by hand.** Open `Context/about-me.md`, read the prompt in each section, and fill in the blanks. Ten minutes.

**3. Connect it** so your AI reads it every session — pick your path below.

## Connect it (so your AI reads your brain)
**🟢 No-code (any AI, 10 min):** fill in `Context/about-me.md`, then paste it at the top of a ChatGPT/Claude chat (or add it as a Project's instructions). Done — the AI knows you before you type.

**🔵 Power (Obsidian + Claude Code):** keep the four folders in [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md), and keep the `CLAUDE.md` here — it tells [Claude Code](https://claude.com/claude-code) to read your brain automatically every session. No pasting, ever.

## The daily habits (this is what makes it compound)
1. **Capture** — something matters → drop it in the right room. 10 seconds.
2. **Log** — end of a session, a few lines in today's `Daily/` note.
3. **Promote** — anything worth keeping gets its own note in the right room.
4. **Teach once** — every correction becomes a rule in `about-me.md`. Never explain it twice.

**The filter:** don't save what you won't need again in a month. A second brain is a decision-maker's notebook, not a hoard.
**The upkeep:** ten minutes a month — archive what's done, delete what's stale. These systems die from neglect, not bad structure.

## What one note looks like
See `Intelligence/decisions/_TEMPLATE-decision.md` — it ships with a real worked example. Capturing a decision *and the why* is what stops you (and your AI) re-litigating it later.

## The advanced layer (optional — this is the "yours" part)
Once you're past a thousand notes, the question is: how does the AI find the right one without reading everything? The tool I use is **[graphify](https://github.com/Graphify-Labs/graphify)** — it turns your vault (and your code) into a queryable map, so the AI reads the map, not every file, and it surfaces connections you never drew. It's a power tool, not step one — add it when your *why* calls for it.
